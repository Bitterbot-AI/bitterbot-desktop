**User:** How does price elasticity work?

**Assistant:** Price elasticity is a measure of how responsive the quantity demanded of a good or service is to a change in its price. It tells us how much the quantity demanded will change in response to a change in price, and it's an important concept for businesses to understand because it helps them to determine how sensitive consumers are to changes in price. There are a few different types of price elasticity, including:

1. Elastic demand: When the demand for a good or service is elastic, a small change in price will result in a large change in the quantity demanded. This means that consumers are sensitive to changes in price and are likely to purchase less of a good or service if the price increases.
2. Inelastic demand: When the demand for a good or service is inelastic, a small change in price will result in a small change in the quantity demanded. This means that consumers are not very sensitive to changes in price and are likely to continue purchasing the same quantity of a good or service even if the price increases.
3. Unit elastic: When the demand for a good or service is unit elastic, a change in price results in an equal proportionate change in the quantity demanded. This means that the percentage change in quantity demanded is equal to the percentage change in price.
4. Perfectly elastic demand: When the demand is perfectly elastic, a small change in price causes an infinite change in quantity demanded. This is a theoretical case, and it means that if the price increase, the quantity demanded will be zero, and it's often used to explain the concept of a price floor or a price ceiling.

Price elasticity can be calculated by dividing the percentage change in the quantity demanded by the percentage change in price. It's a ratio and its value can be positive, zero or negative. A value greater than 1 means that the demand is elastic, a value less than 1 means that the demand is inelastic, and a value of 1 means that the demand is unit elastic.

Understanding price elasticity can help a company to determine the optimal price for a good or service, and whether to increase or decrease the price to maximize profits. Also, it can help a company to understand how a change in price will impact revenue and how much they can increase or decrease the price to maintain the revenue.
