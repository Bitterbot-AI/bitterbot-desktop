**Assistant:** The Negroni: a classic, elegant, and potent cocktail that's not for the faint of heart! I'm happy to help you perfect your Negroni game.

A traditional Negroni consists of three main ingredients:

1. **Gin**: The base spirit, providing a crisp, botanical flavor profile.
2. **Campari**: A bitter, sweet, and sour Italian liqueur that adds depth and complexity.
3. **Sweet Vermouth**: A fortified wine that adds sweetness and subtle herbal notes.

To avoid bitterness, let's dive into some tips to help you strike the perfect balance:

**1. Choose a balanced gin**: Select a gin with a moderate juniper level, as this will help counterbalance the bitterness of the Campari. Look for gins with a more citrus-forward or floral profile, like Bombay Sapphire or Hendrick's.

**2. Adjust the Campari ratio**: The traditional ratio is 1:1:1, but if you find the Negroni too bitter, try reducing the Campari to 0.75 or 0.5 parts. This will still provide a distinct flavor without overwhelming the other ingredients.

**3. Select a sweet vermouth that complements**: Opt for a sweet vermouth with a rich, fruity flavor profile, like Carpano Antica or Cinzano. This will help balance the bitterness and add depth to the cocktail.

**4. Pay attention to the ice**: Use large, cold ice cubes or even a single, large ice block to chill and dilute the cocktail slowly. This will prevent over-dilution and help maintain the flavors.

**5. Stir, don't shake**: A Negroni should be stirred, not shaken, to prevent introducing air and making the drink cloudy. Stir for about 20-30 seconds to combine and chill the ingredients.

**6. Experiment with garnishes**: A classic orange twist or slice can help balance the bitterness, while a lemon twist or slice can add a brighter, citrusy note.

**7. Consider the order of addition**: Some bartenders argue that adding the Campari last can help integrate the flavors better. Try adding the gin, sweet vermouth, and then Campari to see if this makes a difference for you.

Here's a recipe to get you started:

**Classic Negroni Recipe**

- 1 1/2 oz (45 ml) Gin
- 1/2 oz (15 ml) Sweet Vermouth
- 1/2 oz (15 ml) Campari
- Orange twist or slice, for garnish

Stir all ingredients in a mixing glass filled with ice for 20-30 seconds. Strain into a chilled coupe or rocks glass. Garnish with an orange twist or slice.

Remember, the key to a great Negroni is balance. Experiment with the ratios and ingredients to find your perfect harmony. Happy mixing!
